What is the PDF to Word Converter?

The PDF to Word Converter is a free online tool that extracts text content from PDF documents and outputs a fully editable Microsoft Word (.docx) file — all without uploading your document anywhere. Using Mozilla's PDF.js for text parsing and JSZip for building the Word format, the tool runs entirely inside your browser and delivers your converted file in seconds. It is ideal for converting contracts, reports, academic papers, and business documents that you need to edit, annotate, or reformat in Word.

The converter intelligently detects heading structure, preserves paragraph breaks, and optionally adds page labels to the output document. While complex layouts with tables, multi-column text, and embedded images are subject to browser-side limitations, the text content itself is faithfully reproduced, giving you a strong starting point for editing without retyping a single word.

How to Use — Step-by-Step

  1. Drop your PDF file into the upload zone or click to browse and select it.
  2. Choose your conversion options: enable heading detection, line break preservation, and page labels as needed.
  3. Click Convert to Word and watch the progress bar as each page is processed.
  4. Review the text preview to verify the extraction quality before downloading.
  5. Click Download .docx to save the Word file, or Download .txt for plain text.
  6. Open the downloaded .docx in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, or LibreOffice to begin editing.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Is the PDF to Word Converter free to use?
Yes, entirely free. There are no subscription tiers, conversion limits, or account requirements. You can convert as many PDF documents to Word as you need at no cost.
❓ Is my data secure? Does it upload my files?
Your files are never uploaded to any server. PDF extraction and .docx generation happen entirely in your browser using PDF.js and JSZip. When you close the page, nothing persists.
❓ Will the formatting be preserved in the Word document?
Text content, paragraph structure, and detected headings are preserved. However, complex formatting like tables, multi-column layouts, text boxes, and embedded images are not carried over due to limitations in browser-side PDF rendering. The result is best described as a clean text-based Word document.
❓ Can I open the .docx file in Google Docs?
Yes. The output .docx file uses the standard Open XML format and can be opened in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, LibreOffice Writer, and WPS Office. Simply upload it to Google Drive and open with Google Docs for full editing.
❓ What file formats are supported as input?
Only PDF files (.pdf) can be converted. The output is a .docx Word document, or optionally a plain text .txt file if you only need the raw content without Word formatting.
