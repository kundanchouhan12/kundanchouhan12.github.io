What is the Word to PDF Converter?

The ToolKit Pro Word to PDF Converter transforms Microsoft Word (.docx) documents into high-quality PDF files directly inside your web browser. Unlike other online converters that upload your files to remote servers, our tool processes everything locally on your device — your documents never leave your computer.

We use mammoth.js, the industry-standard open-source library for DOCX conversion, to accurately parse your Word document and preserve its structure. This means headings, bold and italic text, bullet lists, numbered lists, tables, embedded images, and paragraph formatting are all faithfully reproduced in the output PDF. Whether you need to share a resume, submit a report, or archive a contract, the result looks professional and print-ready.

How to Use — Step-by-Step

  1. Click the upload area or drag and drop your .docx file onto the drop zone.
  2. Watch the live preview appear on the right — you'll see your document exactly as it will be converted.
  3. Adjust PDF settings: choose page size (A4, Letter, A3), orientation, margins, and image quality.
  4. Click "Convert to PDF" and wait a few seconds while the PDF is generated.
  5. Your browser will automatically download the finished PDF file.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Is this tool free to use?
Yes, completely free with no limits, no watermarks, and no account required. Convert as many Word documents as you need.
❓ Does it upload my file to a server?
No. Everything happens in your browser using JavaScript. Your document is never sent to any server — it's completely private and secure.
❓ Does it keep my formatting (tables, headings, images)?
Yes. We use mammoth.js which is specifically built for DOCX conversion and properly handles headings (H1–H6), bold, italic, bullet lists, numbered lists, tables, and embedded images.
❓ Why does my complex document look different?
Very complex formatting like multi-column layouts, SmartArt graphics, custom fonts, text boxes, and Word macros cannot be perfectly replicated in a browser environment. For standard documents like reports, resumes, and articles, results are excellent.
❓ What file formats are supported?
Currently supports .docx (Microsoft Word 2007 and later). Older .doc format is not supported. Save your file as .docx in Word first if needed.
