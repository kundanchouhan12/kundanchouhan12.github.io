    What is the Image to PDF Converter?

    The Image to PDF Converter is a free, browser-based tool that transforms one or more images into a single, professional PDF document. Whether you are creating a photo album, bundling scanned pages of a form, submitting ID documents, or packaging product photos for a client, this tool handles it in seconds. It supports JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP formats, and lets you combine multiple images into a multi-page PDF with full control over page size, orientation, and image fit mode.

    The tool uses PDF-lib to build the PDF entirely inside your browser, which means no internet connection is required after the page loads and your images are never sent to a remote server. Each image becomes a separate page, and you can reorder images before converting to control the exact sequence of pages in the final document.

    How to Use — Step-by-Step

    1. Click the drop zone or drag and drop your image files (JPG, PNG, WebP, BMP, GIF) onto the page.
    2. Add as many images as needed — each will become one page in the PDF.
    3. Use the arrow buttons to reorder images and set your desired page sequence.
    4. Choose page size (A4, Letter, or fit-to-image), orientation, and image fit mode from the options panel.
    5. Click Convert to PDF and wait while the PDF is assembled in your browser.
    6. Click Download PDF to save your finished multi-page PDF to your device.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    ❓ Is the Image to PDF tool free to use?
    Yes, completely free. There are no subscription plans, usage limits, watermarks, or registration requirements. You can convert as many images as you need at no cost.
    ❓ Is my data secure? Does it upload my images?
    Your images are never uploaded to any server. All PDF creation happens locally in your browser using PDF-lib. Personal photos, identity documents, and sensitive images stay entirely on your device.
    ❓ How many images can I convert into one PDF?
    There is no hard limit. You can add as many images as your browser's memory allows. For very large batches of high-resolution photos, the conversion process may take longer. Modern devices typically handle dozens of images without issue.
    ❓ Will the image quality be reduced in the PDF?
    Images are re-encoded as JPEG at 92% quality to ensure PDF compatibility. This provides excellent visual quality that is virtually indistinguishable from the original while keeping file sizes manageable. PNG transparency is composited over a white background before encoding.
    ❓ What image formats are supported?
    The tool accepts JPG/JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP image files. All formats are automatically converted to JPEG during the PDF generation process. The output is always a standard PDF compatible with all PDF readers.
