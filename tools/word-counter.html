What is the Word & Character Counter?

The Word & Character Counter is a free, browser-based writing tool that provides instant, real-time analysis of your text. As you type or paste content, it counts words, characters (with and without spaces), sentences, paragraphs, lines, and unique words. It also estimates reading time at 200 words per minute and speaking time at 130 words per minute — two metrics that are invaluable for writers, bloggers, presenters, and content creators who need to hit specific length targets.

The tool goes beyond simple counting with a detailed analysis panel that shows average word length, average sentence length, the longest word used, the most frequently occurring non-common word, and a keyword frequency chart showing the top 15 content words. This makes it useful not just for hitting word count targets but for analysing the vocabulary density and readability of your writing — critical for SEO content optimisation and academic writing.

How to Use — Step-by-Step

  1. Type or paste your text directly into the text area — counting starts immediately in real-time.
  2. The counter grid instantly shows words, characters, characters without spaces, sentences, paragraphs, lines, unique words, and reading time.
  3. Scroll down to the Detailed Analysis panel for average word length, sentence length, longest word, and keyword frequency.
  4. Use the Find & Highlight panel to search for a specific word or phrase and see how many times it appears.
  5. Click Load Sample to try the tool with example text, or Clear to reset everything.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Is the Word Counter free to use?
Yes, completely free with no usage limits, account requirements, or hidden fees. Count words in any text — essays, articles, blog posts, or social media content — as often as you need.
❓ Is my data secure? Does it upload my text?
Your text is never transmitted to any server. All counting and analysis runs locally in your browser using JavaScript. Confidential drafts, private documents, and sensitive content stay entirely on your device.
❓ How is word count calculated?
Words are counted by matching all contiguous sequences of word characters including letters, digits, and apostrophes (contractions count as one word). This matches the counting methodology used by Microsoft Word and Google Docs. Hyphenated words like "well-being" count as one word.
❓ What are typical word count targets for different content types?
Typical targets: Twitter posts under 280 characters, Instagram captions under 2,200 characters, blog posts 1,500–2,500 words, short essays 500–800 words, long-form articles 3,000+ words, and academic papers vary by assignment. For SEO, Google favors comprehensive content — top-ranking pages average 1,400–1,900 words.
❓ Can I use this for SEO keyword density analysis?
Yes. The keyword frequency panel shows the top 15 most-used meaningful words in your text (stop words like "the", "and", "is" are filtered out). You can see if your target keyword appears enough times and use the Find tool to count specific keyword occurrences for SEO optimisation.
