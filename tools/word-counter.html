What is the Word & Character Counter?

The Word & Character Counter is a free, browser-based writing tool that provides instant, real-time analysis of your text. As you type or paste content, it counts words, characters (with and without spaces), sentences, paragraphs, lines, and unique words. It also estimates reading time at 200 words per minute and speaking time at 130 words per minute — two metrics that are invaluable for writers, bloggers, presenters, and content creators who need to hit specific length targets.

The tool goes beyond simple counting with a detailed analysis panel that shows average word length, average sentence length, the longest word used, the most frequently occurring non-common word, and a keyword frequency chart showing the top 15 content words. This makes it useful not just for hitting word count targets but for analysing the vocabulary density and readability of your writing — critical for SEO content optimisation and academic writing.

How to Use — Step-by-Step

  1. Type or paste your text directly into the text area — counting starts immediately in real-time.
  2. The counter grid instantly shows words, characters, characters without spaces, sentences, paragraphs, lines, unique words, and reading time.
  3. Scroll down to the Detailed Analysis panel for average word length, sentence length, longest word, and keyword frequency.
  4. Use the Find & Highlight panel to search for a specific word or phrase and see how many times it appears.
  5. Click Load Sample to try the tool with example text, or Clear to reset everything.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many sentences is 100 words?
100 words typically contains 5 to 7 sentences. This assumes an average sentence length of 15–20 words, which is standard for readable prose. Academic writing tends toward longer sentences (20+ words), so 100 words may be only 4–5 sentences in that style.
How many sentences is 130 words?
130 words typically contains 6 to 9 sentences. At an average of 15 words per sentence you get around 8–9 sentences; at 20 words per sentence, about 6–7. It depends on your writing style — shorter sentences are easier to read and perform better for SEO.
How many sentences is 200 words?
200 words is approximately 10 to 13 sentences. Conversational writing with shorter sentences (12–15 words each) produces 13–17 sentences, while formal writing with longer sentences (18–22 words) produces around 9–11 sentences.
How many words in a 5-minute speech?
A 5-minute speech is approximately 650 to 750 words. The average speaking pace is 130–150 words per minute. At 130 wpm, 5 minutes = 650 words. Aim for 650 words for a calm, clear delivery. Use the Speaking Time counter above to check your exact text.
How many words in a 1-minute speech?
A 1-minute speech contains about 130 to 150 words. Most people speak at 130–150 words per minute in a presentation. If you speak faster (160+ wpm), 1 minute ≈ 160 words. Paste your speech text above and check the Speaking Time metric.
How many pages is 500 words?
500 words is approximately 1 page single-spaced or 2 pages double-spaced, using 12pt Arial or Times New Roman with standard 1-inch margins. This is a typical length for a short essay, product description, or blog intro section.
How many pages is 1000 words?
1000 words is approximately 2 pages single-spaced or 4 pages double-spaced with standard formatting. A 1000-word article is a typical length for a short blog post, a news feature, or a college assignment.
What is a good word count for a blog post?
For SEO, the ideal blog post word count is 1,500 to 2,500 words. Top-ranking Google pages average 1,400–1,900 words. Long-form posts (2,500–4,000 words) earn more backlinks and rank for more keyword variations. Posts under 500 words rarely rank for competitive keywords.
How long does it take to read 1000 words?
1000 words takes approximately 4 to 5 minutes to read. The average adult reads 200–250 words per minute. At 200 wpm = 5 minutes; at 250 wpm = 4 minutes. Paste your 1000-word text above and the tool calculates the exact reading time for you.
Is the Word Counter free? Does it upload my text?
Yes, completely free with no usage limits or account required. Your text is never uploaded to any server — all counting runs locally in your browser using JavaScript. Confidential documents and private drafts stay entirely on your device.
